How to read this report

Meta Ads Manager (blue columns)

Purchase and revenue data reported directly by Meta using a 7-day click + 1-day view attribution window. Meta counts a conversion if a customer clicked or viewed an ad within that window — even if they later converted via another channel. This tends to over-attribute revenue to Meta.

ClickMagick — Meta sources only (green columns)

ClickMagick tracks actual clicks and records the last paid click before purchase. The CM columns in the campaign table show only purchases attributed to Meta placements (sources: fb, ig, facebook, an, th). This is a more conservative, last-click view and will typically be lower than what Meta reports.

Blended ROAS (channel summary)

The blended ROAS divides all CM revenue (every source — organic, Google, Meta, and other) by the Meta ad spend. This is the number ClickMagick shows at the top of its dashboard (1.93x). It reflects total business revenue generated during the period while Meta was running, not revenue directly caused by Meta.

Why do Meta ROAS and CM ROAS differ so much?

Meta's 7-day window claims sales that happened days after the ad interaction — including sales driven by email, organic search, or direct revisits. CM only credits the last click. Neither is perfectly "true"; together they give you upper and lower bounds on Meta's actual contribution.
